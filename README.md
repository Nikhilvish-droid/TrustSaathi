# TrustSaathi

### AI-Powered Digital Management Platform for Trusts, Temples, NGOs, and Charitable Organisations

TrustSaathi is an AI-powered digital operating system designed specifically for trusts, temples, NGOs, educational trusts, charitable organisations, and donation-based institutions in India.

It helps organisations convert handwritten registers, receipts, Excel sheets, PDFs, and other financial records into a clean, searchable, and organised digital system.

> **TrustSaathi turns traditional paper-based records into a verified, transparent, and audit-ready digital platform.**

---

## About TrustSaathi

Many trusts and charitable organisations still manage their daily operations using handwritten registers, receipt books, physical documents, and separate Excel files.

These traditional methods make it difficult to:

* Find old donation records
* Track donor information
* Identify repeated donors
* Prepare financial reports
* Maintain supporting documents
* Monitor income and expenses
* Detect incomplete records
* Prepare for audits
* Manage compliance requirements
* Maintain financial transparency

TrustSaathi helps these organisations modernise their existing workflow without forcing them to manually enter years of historical data.

Users can simply upload their existing documents, and the platform helps extract, organise, verify, and manage the information in one central place.

---

## The Problem

Trusts, temples, NGOs, and charitable institutions manage large amounts of important financial and donor information.

However, much of this information is often stored in:

* Handwritten Bahi-Khata registers
* Donation receipt books
* Paper bills
* Physical files
* Different Excel sheets
* PDF reports
* Bank statements
* Records maintained separately by accountants

This creates several problems.

### Difficult Data Retrieval

Finding an old donor record or transaction may require manually checking multiple registers and files.

### Duplicate and Inconsistent Records

The same donor may be recorded using different names, spellings, initials, or formats.

### Missing Information

Important details such as PAN, payment mode, receipt number, address, or supporting documents may be missing.

### Audit Preparation

Organisations often begin arranging records only when an audit is approaching, creating unnecessary pressure and delays.

### Limited Financial Visibility

Trustees may not have a clear real-time view of donations, expenses, available funds, and financial trends.

### Compliance Risk

Incomplete records, missing receipts, unidentified transactions, and missed deadlines may create compliance-related problems.

---

## Our Solution

TrustSaathi provides a single digital platform where organisations can upload, verify, manage, search, and analyse their financial and donor records.

The platform accepts information in the format in which it already exists.

Users can provide:

* Handwritten donation registers
* Receipt and bill images
* Excel and CSV files
* PDF documents
* Bank-related records
* Manually entered transactions
* Online donation information

TrustSaathi processes the information and converts it into an organised digital record.

---

## How TrustSaathi Works

```text
Upload existing documents
        ↓
AI extracts important information
        ↓
Data is cleaned and standardised
        ↓
Uncertain information is shown for review
        ↓
User verifies and approves the records
        ↓
Approved records are added to the digital ledger
        ↓
Compliance checks analyse the information
        ↓
Dashboards, alerts, and reports are generated
```

Human verification remains an important part of the process. Extracted information is reviewed before it becomes part of the final financial ledger.

## Key Features

### 1. Handwritten Register Digitisation

Users can upload photos of handwritten donation registers.

TrustSaathi helps extract information such as:

* Donor name
* Donation amount
* Transaction date
* Payment method
* Receipt number
* PAN or identification details
* Remarks
* Donation purpose

The platform is intended to support regional-language records, beginning with Hindi, Marathi, and English.

---

### 2. Receipt and Document Processing

Users can upload images of:

* Donation receipts
* Expense bills
* Payment acknowledgements
* Invoices
* Supporting financial documents

The extracted information can be connected with the relevant transaction.

---

### 3. Excel and PDF Import

Organisations may already have financial data in different Excel or PDF formats.

TrustSaathi helps bring these records into a common structure so that users do not have to maintain many separate files.

---

### 4. Human Review and Approval

AI-extracted information is shown in a verification table.

Users can:

* Compare the extracted information with the original document
* Correct donor names
* Correct transaction amounts
* Add missing information
* Review uncertain fields
* Approve valid records
* Reject incorrect records
* Request document reprocessing

Only verified records are added to the final ledger.

---

### 5. Centralised Donor Management

TrustSaathi creates a complete donor database.

Organisations can:

* View all donors
* Search for a donor
* View donation history
* Track repeat donors
* Identify incomplete donor profiles
* View total donations by donor
* Filter donors by date or donation amount
* Detect possible duplicate donor records

---

### 6. Digital Financial Ledger

The platform provides a central ledger for managing:

* Donations
* Other income
* Expenses
* Cash transactions
* Bank transactions
* Payment modes
* Receipt references
* Supporting documents
* Financial-year records

This reduces dependence on physical registers and disconnected spreadsheets.

---

### 7. Data Cleaning and Standardisation

TrustSaathi helps clean inconsistent information.

Examples include:

```text
Google Pay, GPay, G-Pay → UPI

Bank Transfer, NEFT, IMPS → Bank Transfer

12/4/26, 12-Apr-2026 → 12 April 2026
```

The system also helps standardise donor names and identify possible duplicates.

---

### 8. Compliance Intelligence

TrustSaathi continuously reviews verified records and generates alerts when information may require attention.

Possible alerts include:

* Donor PAN missing
* Invalid identification format
* Duplicate receipt number
* Missing supporting document
* Incomplete donor information
* Unusual cash transaction
* Possible foreign contribution
* Possible mixing of domestic and foreign funds
* Upcoming filing deadline
* Incomplete report information

Alerts can be classified as:

```text
Green  — No immediate issue detected

Amber  — Information requires review

Red    — Critical issue requiring attention
```

The platform also provides a recommended next action for each alert.

## Example Use Case

A temple maintains its donation records in a handwritten Marathi register.

The trustee takes a photo of the register and uploads it to TrustSaathi.

The platform extracts:

* Donor names
* Dates
* Donation amounts
* Payment modes
* Receipt numbers

One amount is unclear, and the donor PAN is missing.

TrustSaathi highlights these fields for review.

The accountant checks the original register, corrects the amount, and adds the missing information.

The approved records are saved in the digital ledger.

The dashboard is updated, and the system generates an alert for another transaction that requires supporting documentation.

At the end of the financial period, the organisation can generate structured donor and financial reports for its Chartered Accountant.
